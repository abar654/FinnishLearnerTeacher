Resources and Learning Methods

Language learning can be broken up into 4 skills, 2 productive skills - speaking and writing, and 2 comprehension skills - reading and listening. Of course in real life we don't use a single skill in isolation, generally we couple one of the comprehension skills with one of the productive skills to get oral communication (speaking and listening) or written communication (reading and writing).

Finding ways to practice each of the 4 skills outside of classes (and when your Finnish is not developed enough for everyday conversation and media) can be a challenge so check out the resources recommendations for each skill below.
Reading
Improve your reading with books, comics, apps and more.
Listening
Understand more spoken Finnish with TV shows, news, podcasts and more.
Writing
Pick up spelling and grammar and write more athentic Finnish with these tips.
Speaking
Methods for practicing your speaking at all levels.
Since we often use the skills together in real life it is ideal if we can use them together also when we are learning, however, this is not always possible. At the very least we should aim to find a balance between our training of the 4 skills, not just focussing on whatever is easiest or most convenient.

A NOTE ON TEXTBOOKS:

I have not included text books in these lists partly because there are so many, and partly because you will likely just use the textbook prescribed to you when you take a course. Many courses in Finland use the Suomen Mestari series and if you are learning on your own then I would also recommend this course as it is easy to work through without a teacher if needed.

If you want to read more about textbooks there is a nice review of them on one of my favourite sites The Finnish Teacher (note: I politely disagree that Eila ja Ossi, the Finnish Teacher's number 1 pick, is a good text for learning on your own. It is also very hard to find these days).
